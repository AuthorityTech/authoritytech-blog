<h2>Frequently Asked Questions</h2>

<h3>How is AI traffic different from traditional SEO traffic?</h3>
<p>AI traffic comes from citations in AI-generated answers rather than traditional search rankings. When users ask ChatGPT or Perplexity a question, the AI cites sources with links—clicking those links generates AI traffic. Unlike traditional SEO where Google ranks pages based on keywords and backlinks, AI engines cite sources based on authority and relevance. Earned media from Tier 1 publications like Forbes gets cited 5x more than brand-owned content, making earned media the primary driver of AI traffic rather than on-page SEO.</p>

<h3>Which AI platform drives the most traffic?</h3>
<p>ChatGPT dominates AI traffic, accounting for 77-80% of all AI-referred sessions according to SE Ranking research. Perplexity is second at 15-20% with strong performance in U.S. markets, while Google's Gemini accounts for 6-7% and is growing. This distribution means your earned media strategy should prioritize getting cited by ChatGPT, though Perplexity is increasingly important for U.S.-focused brands.</p>

<h3>Why does earned media get cited more than brand content?</h3>
<p>AI engines prefer earned media because it represents third-party validation and carries higher authority signals. Publications like Forbes, TechCrunch, and The Wall Street Journal have established editorial standards and domain authority that AI models trust. Brand-owned content is seen as potentially biased, while earned media provides independent validation of your expertise and value. AI engines cite earned media 5x more frequently than brand sites for this reason.</p>

<h3>How can I track AI traffic in Google Analytics?</h3>
<p>AI traffic attribution requires custom tracking because many AI platforms don't pass proper referrer information. Set up custom dimensions in GA4 to filter for known AI platform referrers (chatgpt.com, openai.com, perplexity.ai, gemini.google.com). Much AI traffic appears as "direct" traffic, so monitor traffic spikes correlated with AI citations. Use UTM parameters where possible and track citation frequency across AI platforms to understand which earned media placements drive the most AI traffic.</p>
