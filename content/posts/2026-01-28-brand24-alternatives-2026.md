<h2>Frequently Asked Questions</h2>

<h3>What's the main difference between Brand24 and enterprise monitoring tools like Meltwater?</h3>
<p>Brand24 offers basic social listening and web monitoring for $79-$399/month with 12-month data retention and simple sentiment analysis. Meltwater provides enterprise-grade media intelligence including print, broadcast, and podcast monitoring, journalist databases, API integrations, and unlimited historical data for $4,000-$15,000+ annually. Meltwater serves large teams needing comprehensive coverage; Brand24 works for startups and SMBs with basic monitoring needs.</p>

<h3>Can monitoring tools help me get media coverage?</h3>
<p>No. Monitoring tools like Brand24, Meltwater, and Brandwatch track mentions after they happen—they don't create them. These platforms alert you when your brand or competitors get coverage, identify journalists, and measure share of voice, but they can't pitch stories, build journalist relationships, or guarantee placements. To earn coverage, you need PR outreach or performance-based earned media services, not monitoring software.</p>

<h3>How do I track AI search mentions with Brand24 alternatives?</h3>
<p>Most traditional monitoring tools (Brand24, Mention, Awario) have limited AI search tracking capabilities. They may catch when ChatGPT or Perplexity mentions appear in web content, but can't directly monitor what these AI platforms say when users query them. For AI visibility tracking, you need to manually test queries in ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Gemini, or use specialized GEO monitoring tools that specifically track AI citations and recommendations.</p>

<h3>What's the ROI of monitoring tools vs. performance PR?</h3>
<p>Monitoring tools cost $79-$3,000/month and provide data—ROI depends on whether your team acts on insights. 89% of brands monitor without taking action, making ROI negligible. Performance-based PR costs per placement (typically $3,000-$8,000 for tier-1 coverage) but delivers tangible outcomes: Forbes articles, TechCrunch mentions, AI citations. Average ROI is 3-5x for performance PR vs. 0.5-1.5x for monitoring subscriptions without dedicated PR teams.</p>

<h3>Should I use both monitoring tools and performance PR?</h3>
<p>Yes, if you have budget and active PR teams. Monitoring tools track competitor activity, identify crisis moments, and measure your earned media's impact after placements go live. Performance PR secures the placements. The ideal workflow: use performance PR to earn tier-1 coverage, then monitor tools to track amplification, sentiment, and competitive response. For lean teams, prioritize earning coverage first—monitoring without placements to track delivers limited value.</p>
